# Audio Frequency Spectrum - Detailed Tonal Characteristics

Comprehensive guide to interpreting tonal qualities of instruments across the frequency spectrum. This reference helps translate spectral energy patterns into specific sonic characteristics.

## Frequency Band Overview

The audio spectrum (20 Hz - 20 kHz) is divided into seven frequency bands, each having a distinctly different impact on the final sound and feel:

### SUB BASS (20-60 Hz)
- **Impact:** RUMBLE, LOSE DEFINITION
- **Character:** Felt more than heard; provides physical impact and power
- **Instruments with significant sub-bass:** Kick drum, bass guitar (lowest notes), pipe organ, synthesizers

### BASS (60-250 Hz)
- **Impact:** BOOM, UNCLEAR, MUDDY
- **Character:** Foundation of the mix; provides warmth and fullness
- **Key for:** Bass instruments, low male vocals, kick drum fundamental

### LOW MIDS (250-500 Hz)
- **Impact:** HONKY, NASAL
- **Character:** Body and warmth; can sound muddy if excessive
- **Critical range for vocal and instrument body

### MIDRANGE (500 Hz-2 kHz)
- **Impact:** GRITTY, LISTENER FATIGUE
- **Character:** Presence and clarity; where most musical information lives
- **Human hearing is most sensitive in this range

### UPPER MIDS (2-4 kHz)
- **Impact:** Contributes to listener fatigue
- **Character:** Attack, bite, clarity, intelligibility
- **Critical for vocal presence and instrument definition

### PRESENCE (4-6 kHz)
- **Impact:** Can pierce and cause listener fatigue
- **Character:** Clarity, edge, "front of speakers" positioning
- **Range where consonants and attack transients live

### HIGH (6-20 kHz)
- **Impact:** SIBILANCE, HISS
- **Character:** Air, sparkle, brilliance
- **Adds dimension and space to sound

## Instrument-Specific Frequency Characteristics

### KICK DRUM
- **30-50 Hz:** BOTTOM END (impact, thump)
- **100-250 Hz:** ROUNDNESS (body, warmth)
- **250-500 Hz:** MUDDINESS (can cloud mix)
- **2-5 kHz:** SLAP (beater attack)
- **5-8 kHz:** ATTACK, SNAP (click)
- **8-12 kHz:** HISS (air, release)

### SNARE DRUM
- **100-200 Hz:** FATS/SOUND (body, weight)
- **300-500 Hz:** BITE (aggression)
- **500-1 kHz:** BARK (crack, snap)
- **2-5 kHz:** SLAP, "T" ADDS PRESENCE (attack clarity)
- **8-12 kHz:** SIZZLE, BRIGHTNESS (wire, air)

### HI-HAT
- **200 Hz:** GONG (unwanted low resonance)
- **300-500 Hz:** BODY (thickness)
- **2-4 kHz:** ADDS PRESENCE (clarity)
- **6-10 kHz:** SIZZLE (shimmer)
- **10-15 kHz:** BRIGHTNESS (sparkle)

### CYMBALS
- **200 Hz:** CLUNK (low metallic)
- **300-500 Hz:** MEAT (body, gong)
- **1-4 kHz:** RINGING OVERTONES (sustain)
- **4-15 kHz:** SPARKLE (brilliance, air)

### BASS GUITAR
- **20-50 Hz:** RUMBLE (sub-bass, felt)
- **50-100 Hz:** BOTTOM (fundamental weight)
- **100-500 Hz:** GROWL (character, aggression)
- **700-1 kHz:** ATTACK (pick/finger noise)
- **2-5 kHz:** POP (string attack, presence)
- **5-7 kHz:** BUZZ (fret noise, string texture)
- **7-10 kHz:** SHEEN (high harmonics)
- **10-12 kHz:** HISS (air, pick noise)

### ELECTRIC GUITAR
- **100 Hz:** HUM (AC hum, unwanted)
- **200-500 Hz:** FULLNESS (body, warmth)
- **500-1 kHz:** PRESENCE (forward positioning)
- **1-3 kHz:** BITE (aggression, crunch)
- **3-6 kHz:** BUZZ (distortion character)
- **6-10 kHz:** AIR (sparkle, presence)
- **10-12 kHz:** HISS (noise, texture)

### ACOUSTIC GUITAR
- **80-120 Hz:** BOTTOM (body resonance)
- **120-200 Hz:** BOOM (can be muddy)
- **200-500 Hz:** BODY (warmth, fullness)
- **1-3 kHz:** HARSHNESS (can be harsh if excessive)
- **3-6 kHz:** CLARITY/PRESENCE (definition, attack)
- **6-10 kHz:** CUT (articulation, pick noise)
- **10-15 kHz:** AIR (sparkle, string noise)

### ORGAN
- **50-100 Hz:** BOTTOM (fundamental power)
- **200-500 Hz:** BODY (warmth)
- **2-5 kHz:** PRESENCE (clarity)

### PIANO
- **50-100 Hz:** PEDAL NOISE (mechanical sounds)
- **80-120 Hz:** BOTTOM (lowest notes fundamental)
- **250-800 Hz:** MUDDINESS (can cloud if excessive)
- **1-5 kHz:** ADDS PRESENCE (clarity, definition)
- **10-20 kHz:** OVERTONES (sparkle, air)

### BRASS (General)
- **200-400 Hz:** WARMTH (body, richness)
- **1-3.5 kHz:** HONK (nasal quality, bite)
- **5-8 kHz:** RASP (edge, aggression)
- **8-12 kHz:** SHRILL (brightness, can be harsh)

### STRINGS (General)
- **200-500 Hz:** FULLNESS & WARMTH (body)
- **2-4 kHz:** SCRATCHY (bow noise, texture)

### SOPRANO VOCALS
- **20 Hz:** POP (plosives, breath)
- **50-100 Hz:** FUNDAMENTAL (lowest notes)
- **150-250 Hz:** BODY (warmth, fullness)
- **4-6 kHz:** PRESENCE (clarity, intelligibility)
- **10-15 kHz:** AIR (sparkle, breath)

### TENOR VOCALS
- **20 Hz:** POP (plosives)
- **50-100 Hz:** FUNDAMENTAL (lowest notes)
- **150-250 Hz:** BODY (chest resonance)
- **2-5 kHz:** PRESENCE (clarity, definition)
- **6-12 kHz:** AIR (brightness, sibilance)

### TENOR SAX
- **100-250 Hz:** GROWL (low-end aggression)
- **2-4 kHz:** BITE (edge, presence)

### HORNS
- **170 Hz:** FULLNESS (body, warmth)
- **6 kHz:** PIERCING (brightness, can be harsh)
- **SLAP:** Percussive articulation

### CONGA
- **200 Hz:** RING (resonance, sustain)

## Caution Zones for Listener Fatigue

Excessive energy in these ranges can cause listening fatigue:

- **500 Hz - 2 kHz (Midrange):** "GRITTY" - ear fatigue from excessive midrange
- **2-4 kHz (Upper Mids):** Contributes significantly to fatigue
- **4-6 kHz (Presence):** "Can pierce" - most fatiguing range
- **6-20 kHz (High):** "HISS" - sibilance and harshness cause fatigue

## Using This Reference for Analysis

### Identifying Mix Balance

**Balanced Mix Indicators:**
- Energy distributed across all frequency bands
- No single band dominates excessively
- Presence range (4-6 kHz) controlled to avoid fatigue

**Bass-Heavy Mix:**
- Strong energy 20-250 Hz
- Risk of "muddy" character if 100-250 Hz excessive
- May lack clarity if upper frequencies deficient

**Bright Mix:**
- Strong energy 4-20 kHz
- Risk of listener fatigue
- May sound thin if low-end deficient

### Detecting Production Issues

**Muddy Mix:**
- Excessive energy 200-500 Hz
- Conflicting low-mid instruments
- Solution: Reduce low-mids on some instruments

**Harsh Mix:**
- Excessive energy 2-6 kHz
- Fatiguing to listen to
- Solution: Reduce presence frequencies

**Thin Mix:**
- Deficient energy below 200 Hz
- Lacks warmth and body
- Solution: Add bass frequencies

### Instrument Presence Analysis

When detecting an instrument in spectral analysis, look for its characteristic frequency signatures:

**Bass Guitar Present:**
- Strong 50-100 Hz (bottom)
- Moderate 100-500 Hz (growl)
- Moderate 700-1000 Hz (attack)

**Acoustic Guitar Present:**
- Moderate 80-200 Hz (body)
- Strong 1-3 kHz (harshness/presence)
- Strong 3-6 kHz (clarity)
- Moderate 6-10 kHz (air)

**Snare Drum Present:**
- Moderate 100-200 Hz (body)
- Strong 2-5 kHz (attack)
- Strong 8-12 kHz (sizzle)

This detailed characterization allows precise interpretation of what's happening in each frequency band of the spectral analysis.
